Spaghetti Bolognese

Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian pasta dish featuring a rich, savory meat sauce made with ground beef, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs. Served over spaghetti, it is a comforting and hearty meal that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 400g ground beef (or pork)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 carrot, grated or finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cans (400g each) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 400g spaghetti
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the ingredients: Chop the onion, garlic, and carrot. Open the cans of crushed tomatoes and have the other ingredients ready.
  2. Cook the ground meat: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the ground beef (or pork) and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
  3. Add the vegetables: Add the chopped onion, garlic, and grated carrot to the pan. Cook for about 5 minutes, until softened.
  4. Add the liquids and seasonings: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, red wine (if using), oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Bring the sauce to a simmer and cook on low heat for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally. Let the flavors meld together.
  5. Cook the spaghetti: While the sauce simmers, bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions (usually about 8-10 minutes). Drain the pasta and set aside.
  6. Combine and serve: Serve the spaghetti on plates, topped with the Bolognese sauce. Optionally, sprinkle with freshly grated Parmesan cheese.
